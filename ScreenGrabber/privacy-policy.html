ScreenGrabber is designed with privacy at its core. All your screenshots, settings, and preferences stay on your Mac. No cloud uploads, no tracking, no analytics.

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how ScreenGrabber ("we," "our," or "the Application") handles your information. Unlike most applications, we've designed ScreenGrabber to operate entirely locally on your device, which means:

  • We don't collect your personal information
  • We don't track your usage
  • We don't send your data anywhere
  • We don't use analytics or telemetry

3. Data Storage

3.1 Local Storage Only

All data created or used by ScreenGrabber is stored locally on your Mac:

  • Screenshots: Saved to ~/Pictures/ScreenGrabber/ (or your chosen location)
  • Settings: Stored in macOS UserDefaults on your device
  • Database: SwiftData database stored locally in your application support folder

3.2 No Cloud Synchronization

ScreenGrabber does not sync your data to any cloud service. Your screenshots and settings remain on your device unless you manually choose to move or share them.

4. Permissions Required

ScreenGrabber requests the following macOS permissions to function. These permissions are used solely for the stated purposes:

4.1 Screen Recording Permission

  • Purpose: Capture screenshots of your screen
  • Data Access: Content visible on your screen when you trigger a capture
  • Storage: Screenshots saved locally to your designated folder
  • Transmission: None - screenshots never leave your device

4.2 Accessibility Permission

  • Purpose: Enable global hotkey functionality
  • Data Access: Keyboard input events for registered hotkeys only
  • Storage: Hotkey preferences saved in UserDefaults
  • Transmission: None

4.3 File System Access

  • Purpose: Save and manage screenshots
  • Data Access: Read/write access to your ScreenGrabber folder
  • Storage: All files remain on your device
  • Transmission: None

4.4 Notifications (Optional)

  • Purpose: Confirm successful screenshot captures
  • Data Access: None - notifications are generated locally
  • Storage: Notification preferences in UserDefaults
  • Transmission: None

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 No Third-Party Analytics

ScreenGrabber does not integrate any third-party analytics services, tracking SDKs, or data collection tools. We don't use:

  • Google Analytics
  • Mixpanel
  • Amplitude
  • Facebook SDK
  • Or any similar service

5.2 Open Source Dependencies

ScreenGrabber is built using:

  • Apple's SwiftUI and SwiftData frameworks (native macOS APIs)
  • Apple's ScreenCaptureKit framework (for screenshot functionality)
  • Carbon framework (for hotkey support)

These are all Apple-provided frameworks that operate locally on your device.

6. Network Activity

ScreenGrabber does not make any network requests.

Zero Network Activity: ScreenGrabber is designed to work completely offline. The app never connects to the internet, never pings servers, and never transmits data over the network.

You can verify this by:

  • Monitoring network activity with tools like Little Snitch or Wireshark
  • Reviewing the source code on GitHub
  • Using the app in airplane mode

7. Your Screenshot Content

7.1 Content Ownership

You retain full ownership of all screenshots captured using ScreenGrabber. We claim no rights to your content.

7.2 Content Privacy

ScreenGrabber does not:

  • View or analyze your screenshot content
  • Use OCR or image recognition on your screenshots
  • Extract metadata beyond basic file information (file size, creation date)
  • Share your screenshots with anyone

7.3 Your Responsibility

You are responsible for:

  • Securing your device and screenshot folder
  • Complying with applicable laws when capturing content
  • Respecting others' privacy and intellectual property rights
  • Managing your screenshot storage and backups

8. Children's Privacy

ScreenGrabber does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. The app is appropriate for all ages when used for its intended purpose.

9. Data Security

9.1 Local Security

Since all data is stored locally on your Mac:

  • Your screenshots are protected by your macOS user account security
  • FileVault encryption (if enabled) protects your data at rest
  • macOS permissions control access to your screenshots folder

9.2 No Data Breaches

Because we don't collect or store data on our servers, there is no risk of a data breach involving your information.

10. Your Privacy Rights

Since we don't collect your data, many traditional privacy rights don't apply. However, you have complete control:

10.1 Right to Access

All your data is already accessible to you on your device in:

  • ~/Pictures/ScreenGrabber/ (your screenshots)
  • macOS System Preferences (your permission settings)
  • The ScreenGrabber app itself (your preferences)

10.2 Right to Delete

You can delete your data at any time:

  • Delete individual screenshots from the app or Finder
  • Delete the entire ScreenGrabber folder
  • Uninstall the app to remove all associated data

10.3 Right to Export

Your screenshots are already in standard PNG format and can be moved or copied anywhere you choose.

11. Changes to This Policy

If we ever change how ScreenGrabber handles privacy (spoiler: we won't start collecting data), we will:

  • Update this Privacy Policy with a new "Last Updated" date
  • Notify users through the app or website
  • Require explicit consent for any data collection

12. Open Source Transparency

ScreenGrabber is open source software. You can:

  • Review the entire source code on GitHub
  • Verify that no data collection occurs
  • Audit network requests (there are none)
  • Build the app yourself if you prefer

13. International Users

ScreenGrabber works the same way worldwide:

  • GDPR (Europe): We don't collect personal data, so GDPR doesn't apply
  • CCPA (California): We don't sell or share personal information
  • Other Jurisdictions: Local data protection laws are satisfied because we don't collect data

When you download ScreenGrabber from GitHub or our website:

  • We don't track who downloads the app
